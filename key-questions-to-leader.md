# Key Questions for a Follower to Ask Their Leader Before a Large Project

## 1. What does success look like?
   - 1.1 What specific outcomes or deliverables define "done"?
   - 1.2 What quantitative KPIs or qualitative criteria will be used to evaluate success?
   - 1.3 What does "great" vs. "acceptable" vs. "failed" look like?
   - 1.4 How will we know we're on track mid-project (milestones, checkpoints)?

## 2. What resources and authority do I have?
   - 2.1 What is the budget and headcount allocated to this project?
   - 2.2 What tools, systems, or third-party access am I authorized to use?
   - 2.3 What decisions can I make unilaterally vs. needing approval?
   - 2.4 What happens if I need more resources mid-project — what's the process?

## 3. What are the constraints and deadlines?
   - 3.1 What is the firm deadline and what drives it (regulatory, contractual, strategic)?
   - 3.2 Which requirements are non-negotiable vs. flexible or negotiable?
   - 3.3 Are there dependencies on other teams, vendors, or external events?
   - 3.4 What compliance, legal, or architectural constraints apply?

## 4. Who are the stakeholders and how do we communicate?
   - 4.1 Who are the key decision-makers, sponsors, and subject matter experts?
   - 4.2 What is the preferred communication cadence (daily standup, weekly sync, written report)?
   - 4.3 What is the escalation path for blockers or critical issues?
   - 4.4 Who needs to be CC'd vs. looped in only on exceptions?

## 5. What risks have you already identified, and what's my latitude to adapt?
   - 5.1 What known risks or past failures should I be aware of?
   - 5.2 What is my tolerance for scope change, timeline slip, or technical debt?
   - 5.3 At what point must I escalate vs. course-correct independently?
   - 5.4 What is the "no-go" boundary — conditions that should kill or pause the project?
